Welcome to the blog!

January 7, 2016

It’s a new year, so it’s time to recommit to overcommiting onself! In the spirit of the season, I plan to begin blogging as an exercise in working through ideas enough to be able to see whether they are silly or worth more thought. I look forward to hearing what others think.

My first stint of writing was taken up by reacquainting myself with github, jekyll, and the hoary workarounds necessary to have fun plugins, such as those that make this page look all Tufte.Thanks to Clay Harmon for the styling. This seems like a good idea in part because I have a bunch of thoughts that I am extremely unlikely to turn into full blown papers, but I think are worthy of fleshing just enough to hear what others think.

Plausible topics for reference:

  • My policy/thoughts on order of authors
  • Why we should cite prior literature, especially across discipilinary boundaries (and what happens when we don’t)
  • Machine teaching: What might this be?
  • Machine teaching: Hard problems of the computer science and psychological varieties
  • Why is this data science?
  • Statisticians have missed the boat. Example N+1: A good algorithm for learning is no good without an effective way to communicate it.
  • Type 1 query machine
